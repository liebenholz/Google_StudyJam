Sesame Crusted Ahi Tuna with Wasabi Mayo

Prep time: 15 minutes Cook time: 5 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ahi tuna, cut into 1-inch steaks
  • 1/4 cup sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon wasabi paste
  • 1/ 4 cup mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. In a shallow dish, combine sesame seeds, olive oil, soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, and ginger.
  3. Dip tuna steaks in the sesame mixture, coating all sides.
  4. Place tuna steaks on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake for 5-7 minutes, or until tuna is cooked to desired doneness.
  6. While the tuna is baking, whisk together wasabi paste and mayonnaise.
  7. Serve tuna steaks with wasabi mayo and a side of steamed vegetables or brown rice.

Wine Pairing: Pinot Noir

Calories: Approximately 400 calories per serving

Nutritional Facts : High in protein, omega-3 fatty acids, and vitamins B6 and D.

Honey Garlic Glazed Chicken Breast

Prep time: 10 minutes Cook time: 20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/ 4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. 2 . In a small bowl, whisk together honey, soy sauce, rice vinegar, olive oil, garlic, and pepper.
  2. Place chicken breasts in a baking dish and pour honey garlic glaze over them.
  3. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until chicken is cooked through.
  4. Baste chicken with glaze every 5 minutes during cooking.
  5. Serve chicken breasts with a side of roasted vegetables or mashed potatoes.

Wine Pairing: Cabernet Sauvignon

Calories: Approximately 300 calories per serving

Nutritional Facts: High in protein, niacin, and selenium.

Spicy Peanut Tofu Stir-Fry (Please note this recipe contains peanuts and should not be served to a customer with a peanut allergy)

Prep time: 10 minutes Cook time: 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 block extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 /4 cup rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toss tofu in cornstarch.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add tofu and cook until golden brown on all sides.
  4. Add bell peppers, onion, and garlic to the skillet and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together peanut butter, soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, and sriracha sauce.
  6. Pour sauce over the vegetables and tofu, and cook until heated through.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve over rice or noodles.

Wine Pairing: Zinfandel

Calories: Approximately 500 calories per serving

Nutritional Facts: High in protein, fiber, and iron.
